Zebulon Montgomery Pike

Early American Explorer
Portrait of Zebulon Pike, oil on canvas by Charles Willson Peale, 1808,
in Independence National Historical Park, Philadelphia.

    Here is a timeline of Zebulon Pike's Life:

  • 1779 - He is born in Lamberton, New Jersey.
  • 1794 - At age 15, he joins the U.S. Army.
  • 1802 - He is commissioned to the rank of officer.
  • 1805 - Under the command of General James Wilkinson at Fort Bellefontaine near St. Louis, Zebulon is ordered to embark on an exbedition to locate the source of the Mississippi River and other military operations.
  • 1806 - He was again chosen for another expedition to conduct peacemaking missions with local Indian nations and get information on a Southwestern boundary with Spain. During the mission, he came upon a mountain which he called blue but was never able to climb and explore due to harsh weather and travel conditions he and his squad faced.
  • 1810 - Zebulon publishes his journals on his exploration of the blue mountain called, The Expeditions of Zebulon Montgomery Pike.
  • 1813 - He dies at age 34, during the Battle of York, Canada, during the War of 1812.
  • 1820 - A man named "James" climbs to the top of Zebulon's blue mountain.
  • 1850 - The mountain is nicknamed, James Peak.
  • 1900 - Zebulon's journals are rediscovered, and he is given credit for discovering James Peak, and the peak is re-nicknamed Pikes Peak.
    • Who knows what other successes Zebulon might have achieved in his lifetime if he had not been a casualty of war.
